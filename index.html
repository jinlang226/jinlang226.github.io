    Researcher, Study and Protection of Ancient Trees in Beijing

    I started this project during middle school. I grew up in a Hutong, an alleyway, in Beijing . In our courtyard, there was a more than 200-year-old jujube tree. Every autumn, the fresh and ripe jujubes would fall on the ground forming a beautiful scene which was the precious memory shared by neighbors. However, due to urban reformation, the tree was transplanted, which worsened its condition over time.
    Inspired by childhood experience, I decided to conduct a project on ancient trees endangered by urban redevelopment in my group. I established information management system and standardizing maintenance. The ancient trees inherit the city's history and connect people. The project on ancient trees is not only my first group work, but also an intention to raise public awareness of ancient trees protection. This project won the First Prize in the 37th Beijing Youth Science and Technology Innovation Contest.
